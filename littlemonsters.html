Industry: Graphic Design


Little Monsters

Is an artistic project that explores the theme of inner decay. Through the use of human faces and bodies as visual support, the artwork symbolises a transformation born out of indignation. Delving into our deepest selves can be a challenging experience as we discover being trapped in mental and emotional mechanisms that control and influence our way of thinking, behaving, and existing. The monstrosity of the ego manifests through changes in our own image, rendering us unrecognisable to ourselves.

Il design riflette proprio questa unicità e assume un aspetto nuovo in ogni pagina, lasciando che sia il contenuto del testo ad informare l’impaginazione. Colori e forme sono stati ispirati dalla bandiera dell’isola di Santa Lucia e sono stati adoperati all’interno delle pagine per creare enfasi, continuità ed identità. Ecco come il libro Mental Health, Well-Being, Breast Cancer & Cancers é diventato un brand a tutti gli effetti che si propone di continuare a divulgare informazione di valore inestimabile attraverso canali di comunicazione sempre nuovi. 


Little Monsters is a digital experiment that exaggerates forms, colours, and meanings with the aim of highlighting the brutal power of the mind. It serves as an exercise of exorcising our internal critic, self-sabotage, repetitive behaviours, depression, escape from our true identity, soul paralysis, self-disgust, and degradation of our own image. This is expressed through an almost obsessive repetition of facial elements, a weakening and obscuring of appearance, and hiding behind a mask that keeps everyone at a distance.

These artworks draw attention to the shadow residing within us, inviting us to reconcile with it and representing an act of love towards the monsters we can choose not to become.

The pieces were created using an old Reflex camera and modified with Photoshop. They feature overlays of portraits, textures, colours, symbols, and words, and hold the purpose of keeping the discussion on mental health alive and emphasising its importance.

They invite us to confront our monsters together, humanising and normalising them, so that we may feel less alone with our inner Little Monsters.
